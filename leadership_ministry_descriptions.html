Leadership Ministry Descriptions

All elected positions are two-year terms, with Governing Board leaders limited to 3 consecutive terms.

Governing Board Chairman

In consultation with the senior pastor, the chairman leads the Governing Board in its mission of ensuring that Wellspring leaders stay focused on our mission and have the resources to accomplish this mission. The Board gives attention to vision, goals, values, personnel, buildings & grounds, finances and any other resources necessary for the mission.

Elder (at least 7, one of whom is elected as the Governing Board chairman)

The majority on the Governing Board, elders lend their wisdom and spiritual maturity to the Governing Board work of providing overall guidance for the church. As a group, elders serve as pastoral advisors, oversee membership and lead in discipline and restoration. Per the CMA constitution, elders must be male.

Governing Board Member at Large

These people have the same Governing Board duties as the elders, vision setting, planning, and decision making on church-wide policy and business issues.

Treasurer

This person, usually a trained accountant, chairs the Finance Committee, disburses funds in accordance with the approved budget, oversees paid staff, and participates on the Governing Board in vision setting, oversight, policy development and business decisions for all the church’s ministries.

Financial Secretary

This officer receives all donations on behalf of the church, keeping records, and documenting the source and purpose for all donations. He/she participates on the Governing Board in vision setting, oversight, and decision making on policy and business issues.

Secretary

This leader records the minutes for Governing Board and members’ meetings, and participates on the Governing Board in vision setting, oversight, and decision making on policy and business issues. The secretary makes sure all the official documents of the church are in order.

Deacon Committee Chairperson

This Chairperson leads the Deacon Committee in ministry of meeting needs and caring for people in crisis.

Deacon (as many as needed, often a team of 15 people)

The deacons lead the congregation in meeting the financial, emotional and physical needs of people within the church family or to people in our community with whom the church has contact. At their meetings, deacons make decisions about meeting specific financial needs and in between meetings, individual deacons take steps to minister to people in need.
